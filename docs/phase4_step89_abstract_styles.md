# Phase 4 Step 89: Abstract Variants

Date: 2026-03-12

## Long Form (~250 words)
We study inverse recovery of sparse higher-order dependencies in latent regime-switching dynamical systems. The target setting combines three difficulties that are typically treated separately: high-order interaction structure, latent Markov state dynamics, and finite-sample noisy observations. We formulate a probabilistic model where regime-conditioned emissions are sparse in pairwise and triplet operators and are learned with variational latent-state inference plus proximal group-sparsity updates. We provide a claim-safe theory program on a restricted but explicit class: identifiability up to regime permutation under separation, incoherence, and non-aliasing conditions; finite-sample upper-bound scaling that separates support-recovery and regime-decoding burdens; and a matching Fano-style lower-bound scaling up to log/constant factors. We also implement a reproducible synthetic pipeline with stress tests, ablations, and robustness checks. Expanded structural sweeps (`N=20..150`, `k=2..4`, `T=500..5000`) show strong high-order recovery in many cells and consistent lift versus pairwise baselines in planted high-order settings. Importantly, explicit `K>=3` switching sweeps reveal a hard regime where current inference remains weak, and we report this negative result as a boundary rather than masking it with threshold changes. The resulting contribution is a rigorous, transparent foundation for sparse higher-order switching-network recovery, with clear scope limits and concrete next steps for strengthening multi-regime recovery guarantees.

We address recovery of sparse higher-order structure in latent regime-switching dynamical systems from noisy finite time series. The proposed model combines sparse pairwise/triplet operators with latent Markov regimes and is trained via variational latent-state inference plus proximal sparsity updates. Theory is closed on an explicit restricted class: identifiability up to regime permutation, finite-sample upper-bound scaling with mixing/separation terms, and Fano-style lower bounds that match at structural order level. Synthetic experiments are fully reproducible and include scaling, ablations, robustness, and baseline comparisons. Expanded structural sweeps (`T` up to 5000) support strong high-order recovery in many settings and clear lift over pairwise baselines in planted high-order regimes. Explicit `K>=3` switching sweeps show current inference weakness, reported transparently as a negative boundary rather than hidden by threshold tuning. This yields a claim-safe theory-first contribution with clear open problems for multi-regime recovery.

## Short Form (~100 words)
We study sparse higher-order network recovery under latent Markov regime switching from noisy finite data. We provide a claim-safe theory package on a restricted class: identifiability up to regime permutation, finite-sample upper-bound scaling, and Fano-style lower bounds with near-matched structural order. A reproducible synthetic pipeline validates high-order lift over pairwise baselines and scaling behavior with `T` up to 5000. We also report explicit negative evidence for harder `K>=3` switching recovery under current inference, treating it as a genuine boundary rather than tuning it away. The result is a rigorous theory-first foundation with transparent scope and open challenges.
